    Thank you for visiting these pages.

    Psychotherapy offers a confidential, judgment-free space, in which you and your therapist will be able to think together about the issues that are troubling you. You will be encouraged to explore freely your experiences, thoughts and emotions, in order to arrive at a renewed sense of understanding, self-appreciation and self- compassion.

    I am an experienced psychodynamic psychotherapist with a background in culture, arts and economics.

    I hold a postgraduate degree in Psychodynamic Psychotherapy from WPF Therapy, Roehampton University, a Foundation Course in Psychoanalysis at the Institute of Psychoanalysis in London, Certificates in Trauma Therapy and Safeguarding and worked as an Associate Psychotherapist with young people and students at a university. I am a certified Reflective Group Facilitator trained at The Tavistock and Portman NHS Foundation Trust.

    I offer both long-term, open-ended work and short-term therapy, which usually lasts 12 weeks.     Fees

    At my practice, I offer both open-ended and 12 week time-limited therapy. The fee for open-ended therapy is £70 per session, which allows for a more flexible and ongoing therapeutic process.

    For those seeking a more structured and focused approach, I offer a time-limited therapy at a fee of £85 per session.

    You can contact me via phone or email to organize an initial 50 minute consultation session during which we will discuss your therapeutic process and your needs.

    Accreditation

    As a professional therapist, I adhere to the codes of ethics and practice of my professional standards authority registers, which include the British Psychoanalytic Council (BPC), United Kingdom Council for Psychotherapy (UKCP), the British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP) and The Foundation for Psychotherapy and Counselling .

    These codes ensure that I uphold the highest standards of ethical conduct and professional practice in my work with clients, and I am committed to providing a safe, confidential, and supportive therapeutic environment.
